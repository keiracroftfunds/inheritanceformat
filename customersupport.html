Withdrawal issue solution

Contacting customer service for any unlisted issues in the 'select options' requires keywords because it's an automated bot that understands only keywords to communicate or give responses to your issues.

Now to communicate, click on the 'headphone' or the 'help' icon, which is the customer service contact.

A question will be asked, "Is this you?" Click "Yes, it's me."

Then, after a response from customer service, you will be asked to select an option below. Regarding the withdrawal issue, you will use keywords so as to communicate the direct issue with customer support. In this case, you will select the " Direct chat " option.



Withdrawal issue.

To check or know the issue with the withdrawal that was made but not credited, type and send the keyword:

" Withdrawal issue "

You will be asked to click on a link and then come back to send "Withdrawal issue check."

Withdrawal issue check.

Click on the link, then go back to customer service again, type and send:

" Withdrawal issue check "



You will get a valid response explaining why your withdrawal is being delayed.

Request refund.

To request a refund of a withdrawal transaction that was made, you will go through the following steps:


Requesting a refund

The system will first have to check the withdrawals you have made that have not been credited; only pending withdrawals can be refunded. In this case, type and send:

' Requesting a refund '

You will be asked to click on a link and come back to type 'i am seeking a refund', click on the link.

Clicking on the link will verify and check if you have an unprocessed transaction.


I am seeking a refund

After clicking the link, go back to customer service again, type and send:

' I am seeking a refund '


You will be given a link to activate a refund. After activation:

1. In your Home/dashboard

2. Go to the Withdrawals box

3. Click on 'view details'

In the withdrawals history, you will see a 'refund' button. Choose a transaction to refund.

Repeat the process if you need to refund multiple transactions.
